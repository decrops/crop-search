This document discusses methods to measure and estimate crop water requirements or consumptive use. It defines consumptive use as the sum of transpiration through plant tissues and evaporation from soil and plant surfaces. Factors that affect consumptive use include climate, soil properties, and plant characteristics. Direct measurement methods include lysimeter experiments and soil moisture depletion studies. Lysimeters involve growing crops in large containers and measuring water and grain outputs. The Blaney-Criddle empirical formula is also described and used to calculate consumptive use and irrigation requirements for a wheat crop based on temperature and daylight hours.
